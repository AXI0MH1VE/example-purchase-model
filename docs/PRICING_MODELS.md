# Pricing Models & Performance-Based Monetization (2025)

## ◈ Overview
The strategy focuses on selling **Outcomes** rather than generic software features. By adopting a performance-based pricing model, we align our success with the merchant's growth, making the proposal high-value and low-friction.

---

## ◈ Primary Tier: The Performance Success Fee (Success Revenue Share)
Recommended for premium brands (Baby, Luxury Furniture, Electronics) with $10M+ in annual revenue.

- **Success Fee**: 5% - 10% of the "Lift" (The additional revenue and margin savings generated by our engine).
- **Lehman-style Structure**: 10% for the first $1M; 5% for the next $4M; 3% for everything over $10M.
- **Example**: If the engine generates $50,000 in additional revenue and $5,000 in procurement savings, our monthly success fee is **$5,500** (at 10%).

---

## ◈ Secondary Tier: The Monthly Performance Retainer
Recommended for brands between $1M and $10M in annual revenue.

- **Base Retainer**: $5,000 - $10,000 per month.
- **Outcome**: Includes a monthly Conversion & Margin Audit, 24/7 technical monitoring of the engine, and algorithmic tuning.
- **Benefit**: Provides predictable cash flow for our team to cover operational/insurance/SaaS hosting costs.

---

## ◈ Tertiary Tier: Usage-Based Metering (SaaS Standard)
For high-volume low-cost brands or automated self-serve integrations.

- **Pricing Lever**: Per 1,000 API calls or per 1,000 recommendations served.
- **Outcome**: Allows for automatic scaling as the client's traffic increases.

---

## ◈ Quaternary Tier: Implementation Bonus
- **One-time Integration Fee**: $5,000 - $15,000.
- **Purpose**: Covers the upfront "technical debt" of mapping schemas and initial engine training with the store's historical data.

---

## ◈ Pricing Strategy Synthesis
Always lead with the **Performance Model** in initial outreach. This demonstrates confidence and results-driven focus. The "Implementation Bonus" should be negotiable to secure high-value long-term performance contracts.
