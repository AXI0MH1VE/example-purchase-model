# Administrative & Legal Setup: Erie County, NY

## ◈ Overview

For a two-person team operating without an immediate LLC, the strategy centers on a **General Partnership** with a registered **DBA** (Doing Business As). This provides legitimacy for bank accounts and contracts while keeping the barrier to entry low.

---

## ◈ Step 1: Registering a DBA in Erie County, NY

Under New York State Business Law Section 130, any partnership doing business under an assumed name must file a certificate in the County Clerk's Office.

1. **Form Preparation**: Complete the **"Business Certificate for Partners"** form.
   - Include names, addresses, and ages of both partners.
   - Specify the "Doing Business As" (DBA) name (e.g., *NMG Partners*, *Axiom Hive Analytics*).
2. **Notarization**: The form must be signed in the presence of a Notary Public.
3. **Filing**: Submit the original form to the:
   > **Erie County Clerk's Office**  
   > 92 Franklin Street  
   > Buffalo, NY 14202
4. **Fees**:
   - **Filing Fee**: $25.
   - **Certified Copies**: $5/copy (It is recommended to obtain TWO certified copies for bank accounts and insurance).

---

## ◈ Step 2: Managing Personal Liability (Insurance)

Since a General Partnership provides NO separation between business liabilities and personal assets, **robust insurance is mandatory.**

| Required Coverage | Purpose | Estimated Monthly Cost (NY) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Professional Liability (E&O)** | Protects against negligence in optimization services. | $70 - $83 |
| **General Liability** | Covers third-party bodily injury or property damage. | $31 - $42 |
| **Cyber Security Insurance** | Protects against data breaches (critical for our SaaS API). | Varies by data volume |
| **Business Owner's Policy (BOP)** | Bundles property and liability for cost savings. | ~$189 |

- **Recommended Providers**: The Hartford, Hiscox, or Insureon.

---

## ◈ Step 3: Business Banking

Use your Certified DBA copies and your Social Security numbers (or an EIN from the IRS, which is free to obtain) to open a dedicated business checking account. This ensures clear bookkeeping for the "Success Fee" model.
